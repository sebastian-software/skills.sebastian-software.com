# United Kingdom

Use this route when the operator, affected people, transactions, device access,
or marketing connects to the United Kingdom. Treat post-Brexit UK law as a
separate route; do not reuse an EU result without checking retained and amended
assimilated and amended UK legislation and current ICO guidance.

## Issue Map

### Provider and company information

Check regulation 6 of the Electronic Commerce (EC Directive) Regulations 2002
for an information society service. It requires covered provider information
to be easily, directly, and permanently accessible and addresses identity,
geographic address, effective electronic contact, public-register details,
authorization, regulated professions, VAT identification, and price clarity.

For companies and LLPs, check the current Company, Limited Liability
Partnership and Business (Names and Trading Disclosures) Regulations. Do not
cite the revoked 2008 regulations: the current 2015 instrument includes website
particulars in regulation 25. Add charity, professional, publisher, financial,
or other entity rules when relevant.

### Privacy, cookies, and marketing

- Map actual processing to the current UK GDPR, Data Protection Act 2018, and
  ICO guidance.
- Check the current text of regulation 6 of PECR for cookies and similar
  technologies. Section 112 of the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025 amended it,
  with the relevant changes in force from 5 February 2026. Verify the current
  consent rule, exceptions, information duty, and effective date rather than
  applying an old EU-style summary automatically.
- Check PECR separately for email, SMS, calls, and related direct marketing.
- Inspect the runtime with [Consent and tracking](consent-and-tracking.md); a
  privacy or cookie page cannot repair premature device access.

### Consumer, platform, accessibility, and sector overlays

For B2C sales or subscriptions, verify current pre-contract, pricing, renewal,
cancellation, withdrawal, and unfair-practice duties. Add current online-safety,
marketplace, age-assurance, accessibility, financial, health, gambling, and
other sector rules only when the service triggers them.
