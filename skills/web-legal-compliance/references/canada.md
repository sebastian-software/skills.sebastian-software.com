# Canada

Use this route when the operator, affected people, transactions, or commercial
messages connect to Canada. Treat federal, provincial, territorial, and
sector-specific rules as separate layers. There is no single Canadian
equivalent of a DACH `Impressum` that resolves all website obligations.

## Research Order

1. Identify the operator's provinces or territories and federally regulated
   status.
2. Identify the provinces or territories intentionally targeted and where
   affected people are located.
3. Map privacy, commercial messages, consumer transactions, language,
   accessibility, advertising, and sector rules independently.
4. Verify current federal and provincial law and regulator guidance. Do not
   rely on a static list of "substantially similar" privacy laws.

Do not route solely by current IP address. Device location, known province,
operator location, transaction destination, and the scope of a particular law
may point to different results. Treat privacy signals such as GPC according to
the verified law and the organization's documented policy, not as a universal
Canadian consent mechanism.

## Issue Map

### Operator and transaction information

Start with accurate legal and trade names, contact channels, business
registration facts, licences, and transaction information. Determine whether
federal or provincial corporate, business-name, ecommerce, remote-contract,
consumer-protection, marketplace, or sector rules require the information on a
site, offer, checkout, receipt, or other record. Do not publish registration or
tax identifiers merely because a global template contains fields for them.

### Privacy

Determine whether PIPEDA, a provincial private-sector privacy law, a
public-sector law, a health-information law, or multiple regimes apply. Build
the notice from actual collection, use, disclosure, safeguards, access,
complaint, retention, service-provider, and cross-border facts.

At minimum, investigate:

- Federal PIPEDA scope and current status
- Quebec's private-sector privacy regime
- Alberta and British Columbia private-sector privacy laws
- Provincial health, employee, public-sector, biometric, and other specialized
  regimes when the data or entity requires them

Name the accountable privacy contact and rights process only after confirming
the organization's operating model. Keep consent interfaces and documented
practices aligned with the notice.

### Commercial electronic messages

Apply Canada's anti-spam legislation to the actual email, SMS, direct-message,
or other commercial electronic message flow. Verify consent or another legal
basis, sender and affiliate identification, contact information, unsubscribe
mechanism, timing, and recordkeeping. A footer on the website does not cure a
non-compliant message flow.

### Provincial overlays

For each targeted province or territory, check:

- Consumer contract, pricing, renewal, cancellation, and refund information
- French-language and presentation requirements, especially for Quebec
- Accessibility duties, including organization type, location, and size
- Advertising, endorsements, contests, loyalty, gift cards, and regulated
  products or services

Do not assume that an English-language B2B site avoids every provincial rule or
that one privacy policy satisfies all provincial notice and consent duties.
