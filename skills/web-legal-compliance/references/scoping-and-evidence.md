# Scoping and Evidence

## Intake

Collect only facts relevant to the requested deliverable. Distinguish facts
confirmed by records from user statements, code-derived observations, and
assumptions.

### Operator

- Full legal name, legal form, trading names, and public-sector or nonprofit
  status
- Registered and operating addresses; countries, provinces, and states of
  establishment
- Registration authority and identifier, tax registrations, and authorized
  representatives
- Public contact channels and the contact responsible for privacy requests
- Licences, professional bodies, regulated titles, and sector regulators

### Service and audience

- Informational site, publisher, SaaS, marketplace, store, subscription,
  community, mobile app companion, or other service
- B2B, B2C, children or teens, employees, patients, students, or mixed audience
- Countries and subnational markets intentionally targeted through shipping,
  sales, currency, language, advertising, sales staff, contracts, or support
- Products, digital content, recurring billing, trials, refunds, delivery,
  reviews, rankings, sponsored content, and user-generated content

### Data and communications

- Forms, accounts, payments, analytics, pixels, session replay, personalization,
  location, profiling, advertising, data sales or sharing, and sensitive data
- Cookie and device-storage inventory, including purpose, provider, lifetime,
  first or third party, and behavior before and after consent
- Email, SMS, push, telemarketing, referral, affiliate, and influencer programs
- Processors, recipients, cross-border transfers, retention, deletion, rights
  requests, and universal opt-out signals

## Applicability Method

For each possible requirement, answer in order:

1. Which operator, service, person, transaction, or communication does the
   rule cover?
2. Which jurisdiction connects to it, and why?
3. Is there a threshold, exemption, sector rule, entity rule, or effective
   date?
4. What exact disclosure, interface, process, or record does the current
   official source require?
5. Where should the requirement be satisfied, and who owns the underlying
   fact or process?
6. What evidence proves the implementation and ongoing accuracy?

Do not infer a rule's scope from its popular name. For example, a privacy law,
anti-spam law, accessibility law, platform rule, and consumer-contract rule can
apply to different parts of the same service.

## Applicability Matrix

Use this structure:

| Surface | Jurisdiction | Trigger and scope | Status | Evidence | Required action | Owner |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Operator identity | Example | Confirmed establishment | Likely applies | Register record + official source | Correct legal name | Legal |
| Analytics consent | Example | Unknown tracker behavior | Research | Tag scan incomplete | Inventory tags before drafting | Product |

Use these statuses consistently:

- **Likely applies**: facts and primary authority support applicability.
- **Conditional**: applicability turns on a named missing or disputed fact.
- **Does not appear to apply**: current evidence supports exclusion; state why.
- **Recommendation**: operational or trust improvement, not presented as law.
- **Counsel decision**: interpretation, enforcement risk, or business choice
  requires qualified review.

## Source Log

For every legal assertion, capture:

| Field | Record |
| --- | --- |
| Jurisdiction | Country and state, province, or member state when relevant |
| Authority | Legislature, official legal database, regulator, or court |
| Instrument | Law, regulation, guidance, order, or decision |
| Pinpoint | Article, section, paragraph, or named guidance section |
| Current status | In force, future effective date, proposed, repealed, or unknown |
| Accessed | ISO date |
| Relevance | One sentence tied to the product fact |

Prefer consolidated legislation and the regulator responsible for the issue.
Check amendment and effective-date information. If only a translation is
available, identify whether it is authoritative. Keep proposals and announced
enforcement priorities out of the mandatory column until their status is
verified.
