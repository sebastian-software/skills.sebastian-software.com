# Czech

Support `cs-CZ`.

## Quotation marks

Use Czech low-high quotation marks: `„Příklad“`. Use `‚příklad‘` for a nested
quotation. Other forms such as inward guillemets are secondary; do not mix them
without a deliberate editorial hierarchy.

Quotation marks touch the quoted text. Place adjacent punctuation according to
whether it belongs to the quotation or the surrounding Czech sentence.

## Dashes and line breaking

- Distinguish the hyphen (`spojovník`) from the dash (`pomlčka`). Use the dash
  for an interruption, relationship, or range according to Czech rules.
- Put spaces around a parenthetical dash. Keep a range dash unspaced where the
  chosen style requires it.
- In carefully typeset Czech, prevent a line break after one-letter
  prepositions and conjunctions such as `k`, `s`, `v`, `z`, `o`, `u`, `a`, and
  `i`. Use a non-breaking space.
- Keep short titles, initials, and number-unit pairs together only where needed;
  avoid creating overflowing unbreakable strings.

## Other conventions

- Put no space before `, . : ; ? !`.
- Preserve the meaning-bearing percent spacing: `50 %` means "fifty percent",
  while closed `50%` is used for an adjectival form such as
  `padesátiprocentní`. Do not normalize one into the other.
- Preserve Czech diacritics and capitalization.
- Use `cs-CZ` locale APIs for numbers, Czech koruna, percentages, dates, times,
  units, lists, collation, and plurals.
