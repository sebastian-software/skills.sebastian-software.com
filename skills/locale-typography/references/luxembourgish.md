# Luxembourgish

Support `lb-LU`. Do not substitute German or French rules merely because
Luxembourg is multilingual.

## Core conventions

- Use `„…“` for outer quotations and `‚…‘` for a nested quotation unless the
  publisher defines another consistent hierarchy.
- Put no space before a colon, semicolon, comma, period, question mark, or
  exclamation mark.
- Use the typographic apostrophe in elisions and contractions: `d’`, `’t`.
- Use a spaced dash for parenthetical interruptions and keep hyphens attached
  inside compounds.
- Preserve Luxembourgish spelling and diacritics. Do not convert the text to
  German orthography while correcting punctuation.
- Format dates, times, numbers, currency, and units with `lb-LU`, not `de-LU`
  or `fr-LU`.

The official rulebook covers punctuation as part of Luxembourgish orthography.
When a specialized publishing question is not covered here, consult it rather
than borrowing a neighboring language's convention.
