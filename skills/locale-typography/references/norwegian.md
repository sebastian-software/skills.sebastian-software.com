# Norwegian

Support `nb-NO` and `nn-NO`. The typographic profile is shared, but spelling
and inflection are not. Never convert Bokmål to Nynorsk or vice versa as part of
a typography pass.

## Quotation marks

Use Norwegian guillemets for ordinary quotations: `«Eksempel»`. Use single
curly quotes for a quotation inside a quotation when needed.

- Put only punctuation belonging to the quoted material inside the quotation
  marks.
- In attributed direct speech, put the comma after the closing quotation mark:
  `«Jeg kommer fredag», sa hun.`
- Keep a question mark or exclamation mark inside when it belongs to the quote,
  without an additional comma: `«Kommer du?» spurte hun.`
- Put the period inside when the quotation is a complete sentence introduced as
  such: `Hun sa: «Nå er det høst.»`

## Other conventions

- Put no space before `, . : ; ? !`.
- Use a spaced en dash for an interruption and an en dash for ranges according
  to the document style; do not substitute a hyphen.
- Use curly apostrophes only where the word or name requires one.
- Keep numeric values with adjacent units and symbols when required.
- Format regional data with `nb-NO` or `nn-NO`; do not use a generic English or
  Danish formatter.
