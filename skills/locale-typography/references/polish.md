# Polish

Support `pl-PL`. Use the official Polish spelling and punctuation rules in
force since 1 January 2026; do not reintroduce examples copied from an older
rule set.

## Quotation marks

Use `„Przykład”` for an outer quotation. Use inward guillemets for a nested
quotation: `»przykład«`.
Put a sentence-final period after the closing quotation mark, including when a
complete sentence is quoted: `„Przykład”.` Keep a question mark or exclamation
mark belonging to the quotation before the closing mark: `„Naprawdę?”.`

## Dashes and line breaking

- Preserve an established choice between the Polish em dash (`pauza`) and en
  dash (`półpauza`) for parenthetical interruptions. Put spaces around the
  parenthetical dash; do not substitute a hyphen.
- Use an en dash consistently for numeric ranges when the house style does so.
- In carefully typeset Polish, prevent a line break after a one-letter
  preposition or conjunction such as `a`, `i`, `o`, `u`, `w`, or `z`. Use a
  non-breaking space, not a visual alignment hack.
- Do not create an overlong unbreakable phrase when applying that convention in
  narrow UI layouts.

## Other conventions

- Put no space before `, . : ; ? !`.
- Preserve Polish diacritics, including uppercase forms.
- Use a curly apostrophe only in foreign names or forms that require it; do not
  add English possessive apostrophes.
- Delegate numbers, złoty, percentages, dates, times, units, and plural
  selection to `pl-PL` locale APIs.
