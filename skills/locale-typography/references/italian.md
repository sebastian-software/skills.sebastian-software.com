# Italian

Support `it-IT` and `it-CH`.

## Quotation marks

Italian editorial practice varies. Preserve the publisher's hierarchy. When no
house style exists, use guillemets for an outer quotation and curly double
quotes for a nested quotation:

```text
«Ha definito il risultato “molto promettente”»
```

Single quotes are commonly reserved for meanings or a deeper nested level. Do
not mix high quotes, guillemets, and straight ASCII quotes arbitrarily.

## Punctuation and spacing

- Put no space before `, . : ; ? !`.
- Use a long dash or spaced en dash for dialogue and parenthetical material only
  according to the established editorial style. Use a hyphen for compounds and
  word-level joins.
- Use curly apostrophes for elision: `l’azienda`, `un’altra`, `po’`.
- Distinguish the apostrophe from an accent and from a prime.
- Keep number-unit and number-currency combinations together when required by
  the formatted result.
- Preserve accented uppercase letters and established Italian capitalization.

Apply Italian punctuation to `it-CH`, but use the Swiss locale for regional
numbers, currency, dates, and time. Do not copy German- or French-Swiss quote
rules into Italian text without a publisher requirement.
