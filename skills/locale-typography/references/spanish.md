# Spanish

Support `es-ES`.

## Quotation marks and dialogue

Use this nesting order in edited prose:

1. angle quotes: `«Ejemplo»`
2. curly double quotes: `“Ejemplo”`
3. curly single quotes: `‘Ejemplo’`

Use the Spanish raya (`—`) rather than a hyphen to introduce dialogue or mark
an aside. In an aside, attach the opening and closing raya to the enclosed
words while spacing the surrounding sentence correctly:
`La decisión —explicó Ana— ya estaba tomada.`

## Punctuation and spacing

- Use both opening and closing question or exclamation marks: `¿…?`, `¡…!`.
- Put no space before `, . : ; ? !` or inside ordinary quotation marks.
- Keep punctuation that belongs to the full sentence outside a quoted fragment;
  keep punctuation belonging to the quoted sentence inside.
- Use the typographic ellipsis consistently. Do not add a fourth period when an
  ellipsis already closes a sentence.
- Preserve accents on uppercase letters.
- Do not use an English comma after the greeting in a letter or email; Spanish
  convention uses a colon: `Hola, Ana:`.

Use `Intl` with `es-ES` for decimals, grouping, currency, dates, times, units,
and lists. Do not translate an English numeric format mechanically.
