# Network and Conversations

Build access to the target market and create useful conversations. Do not treat
connections or message volume as outcomes.

## Find relevant people

Translate the target-buyer evidence into search criteria:

- role variants and seniority
- company type, size, geography, and market
- triggering events or current initiatives
- customer vocabulary and adjacent keywords
- exclusions that remove obvious poor fit

Use current LinkedIn search capabilities and Boolean operators where supported.
Do not assume a paid plan is required. Verify current search limits, filters,
and terms before recommending a tool or automation.

Start with warmer sources:

1. existing contacts and dormant relationships
2. referrals and shared communities
3. profile visitors when visible
4. people who comment on or meaningfully engage with relevant content
5. participants around target customers, peers, events, or groups
6. cold search results that match the commercial thesis

Keep a working list with fit reason, relationship signal, last meaningful
interaction, next step, owner, and outcome. Collect only data needed for the
relationship and sales process.

## Connect and welcome

Use a connection note only when it adds real context. Test note versus no-note
behavior rather than treating either as universal.

After acceptance, acknowledge the context and open room for conversation. A
welcome message should be brief, specific, and easy to ignore or answer. Do not
attach a generic pitch to every new connection.

Useful structure:

```text
context or genuine observation
why it was relevant to connect
one natural, low-effort question or no question at all
```

Choose one welcome objective rather than combining them:

- **Recognition:** Name the relevant topic and offer to be helpful without
  expecting an answer.
- **Permission-based resource:** Mention one directly relevant asset and ask
  whether the person wants it. Send the link only after consent.
- **Visible-need approach:** Address a concrete, externally visible need and a
  credible way to help. Use this only when the need is real enough to justify a
  direct business question.
- **Relational icebreaker:** Use a meaningful shared context to start a genuine
  exchange without forcing a commercial transition.

Select one pattern the sender can use naturally, then test and improve it. More
templates do not compensate for weak relevance or inconsistent follow-through.

## Choose a conversation motion

Use the strongest legitimate relationship signal available:

- **Relevant direct approach:** Connect a real profile, company, or situational
  signal to a plausible problem. Invite a conversation only when the fit and
  timing justify it; otherwise reopen dialogue first.
- **Profile-visit follow-up:** Treat a visible visit as a warm signal, not as
  proof of intent. Offer relevant context without making the person feel
  surveilled.
- **Content follow-up:** Reference a substantive comment or reaction. Comments
  usually provide more context than likes; continue the public exchange before
  moving private when appropriate.
- **Research question:** Ask one genuinely useful question with a low-effort
  response format. Share how the answer will be used. Do not convert research
  into a concealed pitch.

Personalize from relevance, not trivia. Shared geography, education, industry,
or interests help only when they lead naturally to the business context.

## Move from chat to meeting

Use this sequence flexibly:

1. establish context
2. understand the current situation
3. confirm the problem and desired change
4. offer a useful observation or resource
5. check fit, authority, timing, and stakes without interrogating
6. propose the smallest sensible next step
7. make the meeting purpose, participants, duration, and expected outcome clear
8. accept a no and leave the relationship intact

Do not force a booking into every exchange. A useful conversation, referral,
market insight, future trigger, or clear disqualification is still an outcome.

## Operate and learn

Set a daily or weekly capacity that leaves room for research and real replies.
A small number of thoughtful conversations is a better default than mass
activity; tune the number to deal size, sales cycle, and response capacity.

Maintain one versioned message library. For every variant record:

- target signal and hypothesis
- message and degree of personalization
- date range and sender
- sent, accepted, replied, positive, meeting, qualified, and opportunity counts
- objections, language, and qualitative learning

Review the whole path. A high reply rate with poor qualification is not a win.
Change one meaningful variable at a time and stop variants that create confusion,
complaints, or poor-fit meetings.

## Quality Check

- Is there a clear reason this person, this message, and this timing fit?
- Can every personalized detail be used respectfully and explained?
- Is the ask proportionate to the relationship signal?
- Does the recipient understand the commercial intent before committing time?
- Can the team respond personally if the message succeeds?
- Are platform terms, privacy, and direct-marketing obligations satisfied?
