# Content System

Use content to make expertise, judgment, personality, and an offer legible to
the right people. Connect it to conversations and evidence rather than chasing
virality as an isolated result.

## Build the content flywheel

```text
customer cases and conversations
  -> useful points of view and stories
  -> content and distribution
  -> relevant engagement and profile visits
  -> conversations and commercial learning
  -> stronger language, proof, offers, and content
```

Capture new questions, objections, phrases, and outcomes at every turn. Keep
private or confidential material out of the idea bank.

## Use four content roles

- **Thought leadership:** explain situations, questions, decisions, trends, and
  methods where the business has genuine expertise.
- **Growth:** express a defensible point of view through comparisons, lessons,
  corrections, or useful tension that broadens relevant reach.
- **Social:** reveal founder or team perspective, values, working style, and
  credible personal context that helps buyers assess trust and fit.
- **Promotion and conversion:** make proof, offers, events, lead magnets, and
  next steps visible without pretending the post has no commercial purpose.

Choose the role before drafting. Maintain an idea bank populated from customer
situations, repeated questions, market changes, project lessons, strong opinions,
team moments, and business evidence.

Keep role and format independent: any role may use text, an image, a document,
or video when that medium carries the idea well.

## Hand off post creation

This reference owns content-system strategy: roles, source evidence, the
production loop, distribution, and measurement. The optional `linkedin-posts`
skill owns post ideas, calendars, format choice, hooks, structure, hashtags, and
copy. Without it, use the brief below as the minimum drafting contract and keep
each post tied to an evidence source, content role, buyer situation, and useful
next step.

When the system needs an actual post, pass `linkedin-posts` a concise brief:

- target audience and buyer situation
- content role and purpose
- source evidence, approved proof, and confidentiality constraints
- useful claim, story, lesson, or question
- connection to the offer or next step, if any
- voice, language, capacity, and review owner

Return here after drafting to check alignment with the acquisition path,
production capacity, and learning goal. Do not create or maintain a competing
post-writing formula in this file.

## Use AI as an editor and sparring partner

Supply grounded inputs such as the target-buyer evidence, commercial thesis,
case notes, founder perspective, and approved proof. Start from the person's own
idea, bullet points, or spoken explanation.

Use AI to:

- explore angles and hooks
- identify missing evidence or counterarguments
- structure a rough explanation
- generate contrasting versions
- combine the strongest parts
- edit for clarity, voice, and channel fit

Require a human pass for factual accuracy, confidentiality, voice, and intent.
Do not copy a first draft directly or assume a long-lived chat is a reliable
source of truth.

## Plan a sustainable production loop

1. Hold a recurring planning review and assign every item a content role,
   audience, evidence source, owner, and commercial connection.
2. Batch research and drafting when it reduces context switching, while leaving
   room for timely observations.
3. Review proof, permissions, copy, visual accessibility, links, and attribution.
4. Schedule only after the post is ready; do not automate away timely responses.
5. Engage with substantive comments and route qualified conversations
   appropriately.
6. Review performance at a consistent interval and feed learning back into the
   idea bank and offer.

Choose a cadence the team can sustain at the required quality. Do not hardcode a
universal posting frequency or best time.

## Measure by stage

Track enough of the chain to diagnose the bottleneck:

- distribution: relevant impressions and target-account reach when available
- attention: dwell proxies, document or video consumption, and saves
- resonance: substantive comments, shares, and qualified follower growth
- intent: target-buyer profile visits and link clicks
- relationship: accepted connections and useful replies
- pipeline: qualified conversations, meetings, opportunities, and attributed
  revenue or learning

Use qualitative evidence alongside counts. A post with modest reach and one
strong buyer conversation may outperform a viral post with no fit.

## Quality Check

- Does every claim originate in evidence, experience, or a clearly labeled
  opinion?
- Is the post role clear and proportionate to the commercial ask?
- Does the voice sound like the accountable person or company?
- Is the format accessible and usable on mobile?
- Does the review connect content to downstream learning or pipeline?
- Are algorithm claims treated as volatile hypotheses rather than rules?
